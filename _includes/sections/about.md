    <p>I study particle transport in turbulent free-surface flows using laboratory experiments and data-driven methods. My work focuses on understanding how waves and turbulence interact to control transport processes relevant to environmental and geophysical systems.</p>
    <p>While my current work is experimentally driven, lately applying my expertise in the multi-disciplinary cross section of applied mathematics and experimental fluid dynamics has excited me the most.
    <strong>I am seeking postdoctoral opportunities</strong> at the interface of fluid dynamics, applied mathematics, and computational modeling.</p>
    <p>I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Washington in Prof. Michelle DiBenedetto's lab (UW/Princeton) and working closely with Prof. Jim Riley. While at UW, I was honored with a <a href="https://linkoe.org">Link Foundation fellowship</a>. I earned an M.S. in Civil Engineering from The University of Texas at Austin as a <a href="https://foreign.fulbrightonline.org">Fulbright fellow</a>. I received my B.S. in Civil Engineering from Universidad Autónoma Gabriel René Moreno in Bolivia.</p>
    <p>Beyond research, I am committed to expanding access to scientific training. I am the founder of the Science for Bolivia Network, an initiative connecting researchers across disciplines to support scientific education and collaboration.</p>
