    I study particle transport in turbulent free-surface flows using laboratory experiments and data-driven methods. My work focuses on understanding how waves and turbulence interact to control transport processes relevant to environmental and geophysical systems.
    My current work is experimentally driven, but I am actively transitioning toward theoretical and computational approaches to fluid dynamics, with a particular interest in transport, turbulence, and multiphase flows.
    I am currently seeking postdoctoral opportunities at the interface of fluid dynamics, applied mathematics, and computational modeling.
    I completed my M.S. in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Washington and previously earned an M.S. in Civil Engineering from The University of Texas at Austin as a Fulbright fellow. I received my B.S. in Civil Engineering from Universidad Autónoma Gabriel René Moreno in Bolivia.
    Beyond research, I am committed to expanding access to scientific training. I am the founder of the Science for Bolivia Network, an initiative connecting researchers across disciplines to support scientific education and collaboration.
